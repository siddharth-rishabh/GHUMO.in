TRADITIONAL CLOTHING

Chaniya Choli

This is a traditional outfit worn by women, especially during festivals and celebrations. It consists of a flared skirt (chaniya), a blouse (choli), and a dupatta or scarf.

Lugade or Nauvari Sari

This is a unique style of sari worn by women in the region. It involves draping the sari in a way that forms nine (nau) yards of fabric. It is characteristic of the Maharashtrian culture.

Kurta and Pajama

This is a common traditional attire for men. It consists of a knee-length or longer kurta (tunic) paired with pajama (loose-fitting trousers). In some cases, a dhoti (a long, unstitched piece of cloth) may be worn instead of pajama.

Dhoti and Kurta

The dhoti is a traditional garment worn by men in many parts of India. It is a long piece of unstitched cloth that is wrapped around the waist and legs. It is often paired with a kurta.

Pheta

This is a traditional turban worn by men, particularly during ceremonial occasions and festivals. The style and color of the pheta can signify various aspects of the wearer's identity and social status.

Angarkha

The angarkha is a traditional upper garment, typically worn by men. It has a unique overlapping front panel, often fastened with ties, buttons, or hooks.

Ghagra and Kediyu

This is a traditional attire worn by men and women in the region, particularly among the tribal communities. The ghagra is a long skirt worn by women, and the kediyu is a jacket-like garment worn by men.

Bandhani and Tie-Dye

These are traditional textile techniques used to create vibrant and intricate patterns on fabrics. Bandhani involves tying and dyeing, resulting in unique and colorful designs.
